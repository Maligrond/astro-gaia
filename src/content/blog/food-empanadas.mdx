## The History of Empanadas

Empanadas have a rich history that traces back to Spain, where they were originally influenced by Arabic cuisine. The name "empanada" comes from the Spanish verb "empanar," which means to wrap or coat in bread. Over time, empanadas spread across Latin America, each region adding its own unique twist to the recipe.

## Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make empanadas:

### For the Dough

- 3 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup cold butter, cubed
- 1 egg
- 1/2 cup cold water
- 1 tablespoon white vinegar

### For the Filling

You can get creative with the fillings, but here’s a classic beef filling:

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 1 pound ground beef
- 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
- 1/4 cup green olives, chopped
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1/4 cup raisins (optional)
- 1 egg (for egg wash)

## Step-by-Step Recipe

### 1. Preparing the Dough

1. In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt.
2. Add the cold butter cubes and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
3. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg, water, and vinegar. Add this mixture to the flour and butter, mixing until a dough forms.
4. Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead briefly until smooth. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

### 2. Making the Filling

1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
2. Add the ground beef to the skillet, breaking it up with a spoon, and cook until browned.
3. Stir in the chopped hard-boiled eggs, green olives, paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Add raisins if using. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to combine flavors.
4. Remove the filling from heat and let it cool to room temperature.

### 3. Assembling the Empanadas

1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. On a floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to about 1/8-inch thickness. Use a round cutter (about 4-5 inches in diameter) to cut out circles of dough.
3. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each dough circle. Be careful not to overfill.
4. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges together and crimp with a fork to seal.
5. Transfer the empanadas to the prepared baking sheet. Beat the remaining egg and brush the tops of the empanadas with the egg wash.

### 4. Baking the Empanadas

1. Bake the empanadas in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown.
2. Let them cool slightly before serving.

## Tips for Perfect Empanadas

1. **Keep the Dough Cold**: This helps achieve a flaky crust. If the dough gets too warm while working, chill it again before baking.
2. **Don’t Overfill**: Too much filling can cause the empanadas to burst open during baking.
3. **Seal Well**: Ensure the edges are well-sealed to prevent leaking.
4. **Experiment with Fillings**: Feel free to try different fillings like chicken, cheese, or vegetables to suit your taste.
