## The Story Behind Risotto alla Milanese

Risotto alla Milanese is a traditional Italian dish known for its vibrant yellow color, which comes from saffron. Legend has it that this dish was accidentally created by a glassblower’s apprentice in Milan who used saffron to color the risotto during a wedding feast. The result was a beautifully golden and flavorful dish that has since become a staple in Italian cuisine.

## Ingredients

To make Risotto alla Milanese, you will need the following ingredients:

- 1 ½ cups Arborio rice
- 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- ½ cup dry white wine
- 1 pinch saffron threads
- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
- ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Step-by-Step Recipe

### 1. Preparing the Saffron

First, we need to prepare the saffron. In a small bowl, combine the saffron threads with a few tablespoons of warm broth. Let it sit for about 10 minutes to release its vibrant color and unique flavor.

### 2. Cooking the Onion

In a large saucepan, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and cook until it becomes translucent, about 5 minutes.

### 3. Toasting the Rice

Add the Arborio rice to the saucepan with the onions. Stir continuously for about 2-3 minutes until the rice is lightly toasted. This step is crucial for achieving the perfect texture.

### 4. Adding the Wine

Pour in the dry white wine and stir until it is mostly absorbed by the rice. This adds a wonderful depth of flavor to the risotto.

### 5. Incorporating the Broth

Begin adding the warm broth, one ladle at a time, to the rice. Stir continuously and allow each addition of broth to be absorbed before adding the next. This process takes about 18-20 minutes. About halfway through, add the saffron-infused broth to the rice.

### 6. Finishing Touches

Once the rice is creamy and cooked to al dente, remove the saucepan from the heat. Stir in the remaining butter and grated Parmesan cheese until well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

### 7. Serving the Risotto

Serve the Risotto alla Milanese immediately, garnished with a little extra Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of freshly ground black pepper.

## Tips for the Perfect Risotto

1. **Use Arborio Rice**: This short-grain rice is essential for achieving the creamy texture of risotto.
2. **Stir Constantly**: Continuous stirring helps release the starches from the rice, giving the risotto its signature creaminess.
3. **Add Broth Gradually**: Adding broth slowly allows the rice to cook evenly and absorb the liquid properly.
4. **Serve Immediately**: Risotto is best enjoyed fresh and hot, straight from the pan.
